Research Interests

My research lies at the intersection of data mining, database systems, and machine learning, with a particular focus on scalable algorithms for large-scale and high-dimensional data. I develop fundamental methods that improve the efficiency, robustness, and interpretability of data-driven systems.

My recent work explores three main directions:

  • Outlier Detection: Designing oblique-subspace hashing frameworks that generalize beyond traditional axis-parallel methods. My OS-Hash algorithm enables efficient outlier detection over arbitrary data types, including time-series and graph-structured datasets, yielding significant speedups and higher recall compared to state-of-the-art baselines.
  • Spectral Clustering: Developing stochastic optimization techniques to overcome the memory bottleneck of classical spectral clustering. My BSpec framework replaces the O(nÂ²) similarity-matrix construction with an efficient stochastic factorization pipeline, supporting scalable clustering with out-of-sample embeddings.
  • Approximate Nearest Neighbor Search: Building learning-based optimization layers for HNSW graphs by modeling node and edge visitation statistics across queries. This allows dynamic reconstruction of upper-layer graph structures, leading to faster convergence toward nearest neighbors and improved query performance.
